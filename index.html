Tho8.

A Zettelkasten-native second brain that links notes by meaning, not title — and explains every AI suggestion with visible evidence you can trust.

00 · At a glance

The short version.

We watched six knowledge workers lose ideas, forget note titles, and give up on search in under thirty seconds. So we designed a note-taking workspace where capture is effortless, connection happens by meaning, and every AI nudge earns trust with a one-line reason and a clickable quote.

01 · The problem

Ideas don't die at capture.
They die on the way back.

Across every interview, the same loop kept breaking: people wrote the idea down, but couldn't find it, couldn't link it, or couldn't trust the AI that tried to help them with it.

"I know I wrote this somewhere. If I can't find it in thirty seconds, I just rewrite it."

Capture is fast; setup kills adoption.

Every participant used the lightest tool on their phone (Apple Notes, Google Keep, Drafts) — then moved it into their "real" system later. Tags, templates, and required fields at capture time made ideas vanish.

Linking breaks when you can't remember titles.

Everyone had to stop writing, open search, try three queries, and often gave up. The mental model was "link by idea," but every tool asked for an exact page name first.

AI suggestions without proof get ignored.

A "related note" with no reason was treated as noise. The moment the system showed "because: shared citation with Kahneman & Tversky", trust jumped — and the suggestion actually got used.

06 · Insights

The six patterns
that shaped every decision.

Eight affinity clusters collapsed into six durable insights. Each one has a measurable threshold users will walk away from if we break.

01

Capture must be instant.

Zero required fields. ≤2 taps. Everyone captures on their lightest tool first — setup kills adoption, not features.

02

Linking breaks on title recall.

All six participants wanted to link by idea, not by exact page name. The editor has to meet them at the cursor.

03

Retrieval has a 20–30s budget.

Past it, people ask a colleague or rewrite the thought. Search must tolerate natural paraphrase and show a clear next hop.

04

Nudges only in context.

No push notifications. A small inline chip while editing/reading, with a one-line because… — dismissible in one action.

05

Trust comes from block-level proof.

Shared source > 2-hop path > specific phrase > tags/recency. Page-level "related" feels vague. Quote-level proof earns a click.

06

Cross-tool reality is non-negotiable.

Every participant lived in 2–4 tools. The handoff between email/Slack and the main vault is where ideas actually die.

The Trust Ladder.

When we asked "what would make you trust this suggestion?", participants ranked evidence types in a strikingly consistent order. We turned that order into the design of every AI surface in Tho8.

07 · Design principles

Rules we didn't break.

Every screen, every flow, every AI surface had to pass six gates. If it failed even one, it got rewritten or cut.

Zero-friction capture

One shortcut, one field, one sentence. No tags, no templates, no modal barriers.

Link from fuzzy cues

A phrase, a keyword, even a misspelling — the palette meets the user mid-thought. No title recall required.

Proof over polish

Every suggestion carries a one-line because… and a clickable block-level quote. No invisible magic.

30-second retrieval

Natural-language search, answer cards, one-hop helper. If the first result fails, the next hop is obvious.

Focused local graph

Scoped around the active note, ranked neighbors, capped node count. Never the global hairball.

Progressive disclosure

Normal users get simple capture → suggestions → save. Power users get typed links, filters, graph radius controls.

08 · Key screens

Eight surfaces,
one coherent workspace.

A three-pane shell anchors the product: library on the left, note canvas in the middle, intelligence panel on the right. AI never opens in its own app — it lives next to the cursor.

01 · CORE

Main Workspace

Three-pane shell: library, note canvas, insights rail. Inline suggestions appear beside the cursor, never in their own window.

02 · GRAPH

Second Brain · Holistic Graph

Local scope around the active note. Ranked neighbors. AI idea generation from the selected node, anchored to visible context.

03 · CAPTURE

Quick Capture · Cmd+N

Lightweight overlay on top of any screen. Auto-adds source + timestamp. One primary action: save to Inbox.

04 · RETRIEVAL

Quick Find & Ask · Cmd+K

Natural-language query + typed-in-own-words tolerance. Answer card on the right shows synthesized block + exact evidence.

05 · TRIAGE

Daily Sweep · Inbox triage

End-of-day review of everything captured today. One-click actions: Promote · Link now · Archive. Smart-suggestions nested.

06 · CONNECT

Link-by-idea Palette

Highlight text, hit Cmd+K. Fuzzy suggestions arrive with short because… lines. Evidence preview lives in the right rail.

07 · NUDGE

Inline Suggestion

A quiet, dismissible pill appears right after the cursor. Hover shows the exact quote; Enter inserts; Esc silences it for that note.

08 · TRUST

"Why this suggestion?" Modal

Three-step reasoning path · confidence bar · exact evidence cards. Designed so users can verify the AI in five seconds — or reject it.

11 · Reflections

What the capstone
taught me.

Three things changed how I think about designing with AI — and I'll carry them into every product I ship from here.

Transparency is a feature, not a footnote.

Users didn't need more AI. They needed a reason, a source, and a way to disagree. Every time we added a visible "because…", trust jumped in the next usability round.

Speed and friction beat complexity.

The most cited feature across interviews wasn't a graph or an agent — it was "Cmd+N opens a blank note in 200ms." Friction compounds. Every required field is a user you've lost.

Real behavior beats stated preference.

Participants said they wanted rich tags. Their actual phones had zero. We designed for the behavior we saw, not the workflow they described, and the product got simpler — and better.

V2 high-fidelity prototype is live at tho8.app. Round 3 usability testing begins next month.

01 · The problem

Three failure modes
the system kept landing in.

Warp had a brand — a deep navy operator console with one loud lime accent. What it didn't have was the discipline layer that turns a brand into a system. Every team reinvented the same primitives, and every AI agent that touched our code drifted further from intent.

"We had three coexisting design 'systems' and a Figma library that nobody trusted. Engineers were faster reading another team's CSS than asking design."
01 — DRIFT

Primitives forked silently.

Six versions of "button-primary," four KPI cards, two ways to render a status pill. Each PR that copied an existing pattern locked in someone else's accident. No single source meant no single truth.

02 — DOCS LIE

Tokens changed; prose didn't.

When we retired the green glow on shadow tokens, the lint passed at the token layer — but every doc still taught the retired contract. Agents reading the docs faithfully reconstructed it back into the code. The token lint couldn't catch prose drift.

03 — AGENTS BLIND

LLM-first was an afterthought.

Cursor, Claude, Copilot, and Codex all touched our UI every day. None of them had a contract they could read. They wrote what training data suggested — a generic SaaS aesthetic, not Warp. We needed to make the system machine-legible first, then friendly to humans.

02 · The synthesis

Apple discipline.
Warp substance. Premium psychology.

The brief read "Apple, Ive, Rams" applied to a freight Bloomberg terminal. Those aren't opposing aesthetics — they sit at different layers of the same artifact. The synthesis was to stop choosing and start layering.

Layer 01 · Discipline

Apple, Ive, Rams.

The typographic / motion / spatial rules. How the system behaves.

  • 1.25 modular type scale, tight tracking on display
  • 4-pt base on an 8-pt soft grid
  • Hairline borders carry separation; shadows are reserved for floating UI
  • Decelerate, never bounce — UI motion ≤ 200 ms
Layer 02 · Substance

Warp's material.

The palette, density, and signature gestures. What the system is made of.

  • Obsidian #0D0D0D canvas — neutral near-black, R = G = B
  • Spring Green #00FA8A — one role: action / live / success
  • Satoshi for UI, display, and editorial; JetBrains Mono for any number
  • Three signature primitives: Stat, LiveDot, RateTicker
Layer 03 · Psychology

50 ms halo, peak-end.

The micro-behaviors that make discipline + substance feel inevitable.

  • 50 ms first-impression halo — the entire chrome lands in one paint
  • Aggressive hierarchy: 1.5–2× weight gap between focal and support
  • Peak-end micro-interactions: hover, focus, validate, succeed
  • One disciplined accent — adding a second loud color is a brand violation
03 · Foundations

One mood. One mark.
One face.

Three families of color do the work. One typeface carries every role. The discipline is in the restraint.

Color · 3 fams

Obsidian, neutral, accent.

Obsidian #0D0D0D canvas (R = G = B, no chromatic tilt). Neutral paper + cool grays for hierarchy. Spring Green #00FA8A as the only loud color — it appears precisely where action happens, and nowhere else.

Type · 1 face

Satoshi, every job.

One variable family carries UI, display, body, numerics, and editorial. 300 → 900. JetBrains Mono ships alongside for any number — money, weights, ETAs, code, rate streams.

Spatial · 8pt soft

4-pt base, 8-pt soft grid.

Sub-grid stops at 1.5 / 7 / 9 / 11 / 14 close the optical gaps the strict 8-pt grid leaves. Hairlines, not shadows, separate surfaces.

Motion · 5 dur

Decelerate, never bounce.

Five durations, one easing family. Reduce-motion is honored as a primary spec, not an afterthought.

Methodology · the lesson

Every contract that has a TOKEN layer + a DOCS layer must have a LINT on each layer. The token lint catches tokens; the doc lint catches docs; neither is sufficient alone. Vigilance does not scale across 200+ files and 8-week intervals. Automate, fail CI, never trust a social rule to land verbatim.

09 · Reflections

What I'll carry forward.

Three lessons from Lumen that I'll bring to the next founding-design seat — wherever it lives, whatever it's named, whatever it's measuring.

Layer aesthetics, don't compromise them.

"Apple-clean for a Bloomberg terminal" isn't a contradiction — it's a stacking instruction. Discipline owns typography, motion, and spacing. The brand owns palette, density, and signature gestures. When they pull on different layers, the synthesis stacks.

Vigilance is the wrong currency.

Design systems with >50 contributors and >100 files drift faster than humans can patrol. Replace social rules with lint scripts, CI gates, and lockstep release scripts. Every contract gets two enforcers: one for the source, one for the prose.

Make it legible to agents first.

Most of the code that touches modern UI is now written by — or in close partnership with — an AI agent. A design system whose first reader is an LLM gets better outputs from the agent and the human. md for humans, json for agents, both kept in lockstep. The schema is the system.
