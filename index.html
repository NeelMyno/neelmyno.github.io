Design Process

1

We start with people

First things first, we understand our user's needs, motivations, and goals.

2

We discover & define the problems

Then we dive deep into discovering and defining their problem by conducting research and gathering insights about them.

3

We sketch & whiteboard our ideas

Then we come to the exciting part - we start sketching and whiteboarding our ideas, getting them out of our heads and onto paper.

4

We wireframe our selected solutions

From sketching, we move on to wireframing out selected solutions, building the foundation of the user experience.

5

We create hi-fi prototypes & test those solutions

Finally, we create hi-fi prototypes and test those solutions with real users, iterating until we've created a truly exceptional product.

About Neel

Hi, I'm Neel, a UX/UI Designer from the bustling city of Indore, located in the west-central region of India. My journey started as a computer science graduate. Still, I quickly realized I was more interested in understanding the "why" behind coding. This led me to delve deeper into UI and UX, eventually developing a passion for designing experiences that positively impact users' lives.

With over 3½ years of work experience in UX/UI design, I've honed my skills in communication, organization, and collaboration with developers. I've worked in various industries, including video, animation, graphics, 3D design, web and app interfaces, and design research. This diverse experience has enabled me to ensure user needs align with business goals, ensuring that the final product is aesthetically pleasing but also functional and user-friendly.

Aside from design, I have a few other passions in life. I'm a massive automobile enthusiast, and I love studying and exploring different types of vehicles. I've been a gamer for over five years, and it has been a great stress reliever and a fantastic learning tool. Gaming has improved my strategic thinking, negotiation, management skills, and attention to detail. I'm also a 3D design experimenter, constantly exploring the space between what is real and what is not.

Listening to music isolates me in my environment, which helps me focus on tasks. Whether I'm working on a complex design project or just need to relax, music helps me get in the zone.
