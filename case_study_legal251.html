The Project

Product overview

With Legal251, people can book their lawyer, CA, CS and legal consultant from home. Legal251 provides online service to people who want their legal and financial problems to be solved in no time and at the most affordable cost.

I was responsible for the entire UI and UX design of this project, including a landing page, user-side control panel, admin-side control panel, and an android application.

My role

One-person team

Methods used

Surveys, Interviews, User personas, Market research & Analysis, Affinity mapping, Preference test, Wireframes, Prototyping

Timeline

Industry

The Problem

We aimed to tackle these problems:

  • In business, legal & financial matters feel intimidating to most small and medium business owners. When it comes to documentation, it is hard for them to schedule a meeting with their CAs and lawyers for these works.
    ‍
  • Private legal consultants charge high fees to solve minute problems and assist them with legal and financial issues.
    ‍
  • Carrying documents and papers has always been difficult, and travelling with them is even more.

Process

Background

Hypothesis:

A web solution serves people who want to book their legal consultants without checking in offices. People can upload their documents on the portal for further legal processes and get consultancy on video and audio calls at a low cost.

Business goals:

  • Market Growth: There is a growing online market for legal services. It emerges as a viable opportunity. Day by day, there is an increase in demand. However, the global legal services market size is anticipated to reach USD 1,045.24 billion by 2025, exhibiting a CAGR of 4.4% over the forecast period, according to a new report by Radiant Insights, Inc. The growing use of the latest technologies in offering legal services is expected to drive market growth.
    ‍
  • Communicate Legal251's value proposition: Throughout the years, Legal251 has to be scaled beyond legal consultancies.
    ‍
  • Drive users to Legal251: Attract potential users looking for trusted legal consultancy and help with their legal and financial matters.

Research

Survey results

First, I surveyed 63 people who are local business owners (grocery stores, stationery shops, bakery shops, etc).

User interviews

Who I interview? I interviewed 7 individual business owners who are running small and medium scale businesses full time.

I asked questions about:

  • Their feeling about visiting CA's or lawyer's offices for work.
    ‍
  • Challenges in understanding documents related to legal and finance.
    ‍
  • Understanding legal processes related to tax filings and various business licenses.
    ‍
  • Their feelings about online services facilitate those experiences.

Insights

Main learnings

  • People do not like to visit offices too often for their business-related legal work.
    ‍
  • The majority of people face difficulty in understanding the legal process and documentation.
    ‍
  • About two-thirds prefer to get their solutions over video or audio calls and transfer documents online.
    ‍
  • People are flustered by maintaining deadlines of tax filings and other legal processes.

Since one of the key findings from my user research was that users of varying surf skill levels have different needs, I created two user personas for a beginner and intermediate level surfer. I revisited these user personas often to remind myself of my users' needs and frustrations and maintain a user-centric focus for the duration of the project.

Next steps

As a one-person team, if I decided to pursue the idea in the future, I would have to perform extensive research and more in-depth interviews to understand the users and the landscape better. I would need to build relations with the users, pursue possible outsourcing and collaboration possibilities with similar, existing startups, and conduct multiple rounds of testing before implementation.

Also, from here, I would like to dive deeper into creating a seamless and easily understandable service tracking process for users to follow their orders.

Retrospective

Challenges

  • My biggest challenge was the lack of support that we got from our team as a UX designer because I worked as the one-person team in the Legal251 website project.
    ‍
  • It was hard to make complicated choices without a good team present around you, and workload is also one of the most significant challenging factors.
    ‍
  • As a one-person team, I spent too much time deciding on ideas initially; I decided that while it was essential to take charge, it was equally essential to let stakeholders pitch their ideas. In that attempt, I lost much time that could have been used to refine the idea for our mentor meeting at the end of the day.

What can be improved?

  • I only made use of Adobe XD for this project - everything from my wireframes and prototypes to creating illustrations and icons. While I was able to complete my full design within XD, it is helpful to have a working knowledge of all of the standard tools in the industry. In the future, I would like to expand upon the programs that I use.
    ‍
  • Next time, I would implement a strict schedule that I could follow; this would help keep me on track and focused on the task at hand.
    ‍
  • Additionally, I would have liked to conduct more interviews to verify our assumptions and speak to even more mentors to improve the solution.

What's next?

  • I spent much time researching UI best practices toward the end of this project, and I believe that Legal251 will benefit significantly from a UI rework after completing a UI specialization course with the Google UX Design program.
    ‍
  • I also have a few features that are not yet available on the clickable prototype that I would like to add in the future.

Would I do this again?

  • Absolutely! This was a valuable experience that allowed me to practice my UX skills, network with knowledgeable individuals, and push myself to think outside of the box.
